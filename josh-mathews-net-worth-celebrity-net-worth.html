What is Josh Lomberger's Net Worth?

Josh Lomberger a.k.a. Josh Matthews is an American presenter, announcer and former professional wrestler who has a net worth of $1 million. Born November 25, 1980 in Sea Isle City, New Jersey, he is best known for his work within the WWE (World Wrestling Entertainment). When Lomberger was hired by World Wrestling Entertainment (WWE), he began using the name Josh Matthews. In December 2002, he appeared as the backstage interviewer for the SmackDown! brand and as an announcer on Velocity. He also hosted the WWE webcast Byte This! after Kevin Kelly was released and went on to host several shows on WWE's official website, including Weekly Top 5, The WWE Experience and After Burn. Mathews also filled in for an absent Todd Grisham on a summer 2006 episode of Raw. He hosted pre-shows for WWE pay-per-view events, and provided color commentary on Heat from time to time before the show was canceled in May 2008. He was the host of WWE.com's Weekly Top Five in its beginnings and hosted it until April 2009. On the April 7, 2009 episode of ECW, Mathews was announced as the new play-by-play commentator. Mathews was also the play-by-play commentator of Saturday Morning Slam throughout the show's 2012-2013 run. On September 27, 2013, he started as commentator of Superstars with Tom Phillips and on the October 18, 2013 edition of SmackDown, Alberto Del Rio attacked Mathews while threatening John Cena about what he was going to do to him in Hell in a Cell. On June 25, 2014, Mathews was released from WWE.
